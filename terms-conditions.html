2. Delivery & Site Accessibility

Material logistics depend significantly on site coordinates and vehicle accessibility constraints:

  • Access Pathways: The buyer is solely responsible for ensuring clear, safe, and navigable approach roads for multi-axle dumpers and concrete transit mixers to enter the pouring or dumping location.
  • Unloading Timings: Concrete transit mixers must be unloaded within the initial setting limit (usually 2 hours from batching). Delays on site leading to concrete setting in mixer drums are subject to cleanout penalties.
  • Permits: The buyer must procure all municipal site pouring permissions or local traffic clearance certifications required to operate heavy vehicles.

4. Testing and Material Acceptance

At Manisha Infracon, quality verification is standard:

Aggregates must be checked for grading and dust properties at the time of delivery. Once aggregates are dumped on site, returns based on sizing are not accepted. For RMC deliveries, concrete test cubes must be cast on site by joint supervision. Cube compressive checks must be performed in certified laboratories in accordance with standard codes. We do not accept liability for structural integrity issues stemming from improper curing, formwork failures, or custom water dilution on site.
